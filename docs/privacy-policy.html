Privacy Policy

Last Updated: April 1, 2026

Introduction

QuickDock ("we," "our," or "us") is committed to protecting your privacy. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and safeguard your information when you use the QuickDock Chrome extension.

Information We Collect

QuickDock is designed to be a privacy-first tool. Most of your data stays exactly where it belongs: with you.

  • Google User Data: To provide its core functionality, QuickDock requests access to your Gmail messages via the gmail.readonly scope. Specifically, the extension searches for and reads automated notification emails sent from no-reply@quickscrum.com.
  • Local Storage: We store parsed ticket and story information in your browser's local storage to provide a fast, offline-capable timeline view.
  • Settings: Your configuration settings (such as search limits and URL templates) are stored using Chrome's sync storage to persist across your devices.

How We Use Your Data

The data we access is used exclusively to provide the features of the QuickDock extension:

  • To extract and display your Quickscrum assignments (tickets and user stories) in a centralized hub.
  • To allow you to search and filter your assignments locally within the extension.
  • To navigate directly to your workspace items.

Limited Use Policy: QuickDock's use and transfer to any other app of information received from Google APIs will adhere to the Google API Services User Data Policy, including the Limited Use requirements.

Data Sharing and Disclosure

We do not share, sell, or trade your personal information or Google user data with third parties. All processing of email content is performed locally within your browser extension. No data is sent to external servers owned by us or any other third party (except for communication with Google's own APIs to fetch your data).

Security

Your data is protected by the security measures provided by Google and the Chrome browser. We do not store your Google credentials; authentication is handled entirely through Google's OAuth 2.0 system.

Your Rights

You have full control over your data:

  • You can disconnect the extension from your Google account at any time via your Google Account security settings.
  • You can clear all locally cached data through the extension's settings page.
  • Uninstalling the extension will remove all locally stored data.

Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. Any changes will be reflected by the "Last Updated" date at the top of this page.
